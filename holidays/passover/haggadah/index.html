How To Use This Page

Keep this page open during the Seder. The order follows the traditional 15 steps. Every blessing card includes: how to do it, what is being blessed, and the full text in three formats.

  • Stand for Kiddush and many opening blessings.
  • Recline left when drinking the 4 cups and eating matzah at key points.
  • Do not recline when eating maror.

Important Notes

Customs vary by community. This page gives a reliable, practical structure; follow your family/rabbi for custom text and pronunciation details.

  • Cup size: minimum about 3 oz (86 ml) per cup.
  • Matzah: eat the required amount quickly, ideally within a few minutes.
  • Language: you can fulfill core parts in any language you understand.

Before The Seder

Passover Preparation

הֲכָנוֹת לְפֶסַח

These are the key pre-Seder blessings and declarations so your table starts clean, organized, and spiritually focused.

Checklist

  • Remove/sell chametz.
  • Prepare Seder plate items.
  • Set 4 cups for each participant.
  • Prepare matzah portions and afikoman bag.

Seder Plate Core Items

  • Zeroa (shank bone), Beitzah (egg)
  • Maror, Chazeret (if used)
  • Charoset, Karpas
  • Salt water

Bedikat Chametz Blessing

How: Night before Passover, before searching for chametz by candle/flashlight.

What we are blessing: The mitzvah of removing leaven from our possession.

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם אֲשֶׁר קִדְּשָׁנוּ בְּמִצְוֹתָיו וְצִוָּנוּ עַל בִּעוּר חָמֵץ׃

Transliteration: Barukh atah Adonai, Eloheinu melekh ha-olam, asher kidshanu b'mitzvotav v'tzivanu al bi'ur chametz.

English: Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who sanctified us with His commandments and commanded us regarding the removal of chametz.

Nullification After Search (Kol Chamira)

How: Recite immediately after the nighttime search.

What this declaration does: Any hidden chametz is considered ownerless and null.

כָּל חֲמִירָא וַחֲמִיעָא דְאִכָּא בִרְשׁוּתִי, דְלָא חֲמִתֵּיהּ וּדְלָא בִעַרְתֵּיהּ וּדְלָא יְדַעְנָא לֵיהּ, לִבָּטֵל וְלֶהֱוֵי הֶפְקֵר כְּעַפְרָא דְאַרְעָא׃

Transliteration: Kol chamira va-chamia d'ika virshuti, d'la chazitei u'dla vi'artei u'dla yedana lei, livatel v'lehevei hefker k'afka d'ara.

English: All leaven in my possession that I did not see, remove, or know about shall be nullified and ownerless like the dust of the earth.

Nullification After Burning (Kol Chamira)

How: Recite after burning chametz on Erev Pesach morning.

What this declaration does: Cancels all chametz, whether seen or unseen.

כָּל חֲמִירָא וַחֲמִיעָא דְאִכָּא בִרְשׁוּתִי, דַחֲזִיתֵיהּ וּדְלָא חֲזִיתֵיהּ, דַחֲמִיתֵיהּ וּדְלָא חֲמִיתֵיהּ, דְבִעַרְתֵּיהּ וּדְלָא בִעַרְתֵּיהּ, לִבָּטֵל וְלֶהֱוֵי הֶפְקֵר כְּעַפְרָא דְאַרְעָא׃

Transliteration: Kol chamira va-chamia d'ika virshuti, dachazitei u'dla chazitei, dachamitei u'dla chamitei, d'vi'artei u'dla vi'artei, livatel v'lehevei hefker k'afka d'ara.

English: All leaven in my possession, whether seen or unseen, removed or not removed, shall be nullified and ownerless like the dust of the earth.

Candle Lighting (Yom Tov)

How: Recited before lighting holiday candles (or after on Yom Tov night from an existing flame, according to custom).

What we are blessing: The mitzvah to kindle the festival lights.

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם אֲשֶׁר קִדְּשָׁנוּ בְּמִצְוֹתָיו וְצִוָּנוּ לְהַדְלִיק נֵר שֶׁל יוֹם טוֹב׃

Transliteration: Barukh atah Adonai, Eloheinu melekh ha-olam, asher kidshanu b'mitzvotav v'tzivanu l'hadlik ner shel yom tov.

English: Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who sanctified us with His commandments and commanded us to kindle the festival light.

Shehecheyanu (First Night(s))

How: Said at candle lighting and/or Kiddush (depending on custom and night).

What we are blessing: Gratitude for reaching this special time.

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם שֶׁהֶחֱיָנוּ וְקִיְּמָנוּ וְהִגִּיעָנוּ לַזְּמַן הַזֶּה׃

Transliteration: Barukh atah Adonai, Eloheinu melekh ha-olam, shehecheyanu v'kiy'manu v'higi'anu la-z'man ha-zeh.

English: Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who has kept us alive, sustained us, and brought us to this time.

Eruv Tavshilin (When Yom Tov leads into Shabbat)

How: Recite before Yom Tov if needed so cooking for Shabbat from Yom Tov is permitted.

What we are blessing: The mitzvah of establishing an eruv tavshilin.

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם אֲשֶׁר קִדְּשָׁנוּ בְּמִצְוֹתָיו וְצִוָּנוּ עַל מִצְוַת עֵרוּב׃

Transliteration: Barukh atah Adonai, Eloheinu melekh ha-olam, asher kidshanu b'mitzvotav v'tzivanu al mitzvat eruv.

English: Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who sanctified us with His commandments and commanded us regarding the mitzvah of eruv.

The 15 Steps of the Seder
1

Step 1

Kadesh

קַדֵּשׁ

Kiddush over the first cup of wine.

How to do this step: Fill cup #1. Stand. Recite Kiddush. Then sit, recline to the left, and drink most or all of the cup.

Borei Pri HaGafen (Wine Blessing)

What we are blessing: God as creator of the fruit of the vine.

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם בּוֹרֵא פְּרִי הַגָּפֶן׃

Transliteration: Barukh atah Adonai, Eloheinu melekh ha-olam, borei p'ri ha-gafen.

English: Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, Creator of the fruit of the vine.

Festival Kiddush (Main Sanctification)

What we are blessing: God who chose Israel, sanctified us, and gave us this Festival of Matzot.

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ, אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם אֲשֶׁר בָּחַר בָּנוּ מִכָּל־עָם וְרוֹמְמָנוּ מִכָּל־לָשׁוֹן וְקִדְּשָׁנוּ בְּמִצְוֹתָיו. וַתִּתֶּן לָנוּ יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ בְּאַהֲבָה (לשבת: שַׁבָּתוֹת לִמְנוּחָה וּ)מוֹעֲדִים לְשִׂמְחָה, חַגִּים וּזְמַנִּים לְשָׂשׂוֹן, (לשבת: אֶת יוֹם הַשַּׁבָּת הַזֶּה וְ)אֶת יוֹם חַג הַמַּצּוֹת הַזֶּה זְמַן חֵרוּתֵנוּ, (לשבת: בְּאַהֲבָה) מִקְרָא קֹדֶשׁ זֵכֶר לִיצִיאַת מִצְרָיִם. כִּי בָנוּ בָחַרְתָּ וְאוֹתָנוּ קִדַּשְׁתָּ מִכָּל הָעַמִּים, (לשבת: וְשַׁבָּת) וּמוֹעֲדֵי קָדְשֶׁךָ (לשבת: בְּאַהֲבָה וּבְרָצוֹן) בְּשִׂמְחָה וּבְשָׂשׂוֹן הִנְחַלְתָּנוּ. בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ, מְקַדֵּשׁ (לשבת: הַשַׁבָּת וְ)יִשְׂרָאֵל וְהַזְּמַנִּים׃

Transliteration: Barukh atah Adonai, Eloheinu melekh ha-olam, asher bachar banu mikol am v'rommemanu mikol lashon v'kidshanu b'mitzvotav. Va-titen lanu Adonai Eloheinu b'ahavah mo'adim l'simchah, chagim u-z'manim l'sason, et yom chag ha-matzot ha-zeh z'man cheiruteinu, mikra kodesh zecher l'yetziat Mitzrayim. Ki vanu vacharta v'otanu kidashta mikol ha-amim, u-mo'adei kodsh'cha b'simchah u-v'sason hinchaltanu. Barukh atah Adonai, m'kadesh Yisrael v'ha-z'manim.

English: Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who chose us, sanctified us with mitzvot, and gave us this Festival of Matzot as a holy convocation in memory of the Exodus from Egypt. Blessed are You, Lord, who sanctifies Israel and the festivals.

Shehecheyanu

What we are blessing: Gratitude for reaching this festival moment.

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם שֶׁהֶחֱיָנוּ וְקִיְּמָנוּ וְהִגִּיעָנוּ לַזְּמַן הַזֶּה׃

Transliteration: Barukh atah Adonai, Eloheinu melekh ha-olam, shehecheyanu v'kiy'manu v'higi'anu la-z'man ha-zeh.

English: Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who has kept us alive, sustained us, and brought us to this time.

If Seder begins on Saturday night (Motzaei Shabbat)

Borei Meorei HaEish

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם בּוֹרֵא מְאוֹרֵי הָאֵשׁ׃

Transliteration: Barukh atah Adonai, Eloheinu melekh ha-olam, borei me'orei ha-esh.

English: Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, Creator of the lights of fire.

Havdalah for Yom Tov After Shabbat

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ, אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם, הַמַּבְדִיל בֵּין קֹדֶשׁ לְחֹל, בֵּין אוֹר לְחֹשֶׁךְ, בֵּין יִשְׂרָאֵל לָעַמִּים, בֵּין יוֹם הַשְּׁבִיעִי לְשֵׁשֶׁת יְמֵי הַמַּעֲשֶׂה. בֵּין קְדֻשַּׁת שַׁבָּת לִקְדֻשַּׁת יוֹם טוֹב הִבְדַּלְתָּ, וְאֶת־יוֹם הַשְּׁבִיעִי מִשֵּׁשֶׁת יְמֵי הַמַּעֲשֶׂה קִדַּשְׁתָּ. הִבְדַּלְתָּ וְקִדַּשְׁתָּ אֶת־עַמְּךָ יִשְׂרָאֵל בִּקְדֻשָּׁתֶךָ. בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ, הַמַּבְדִּיל בֵּין קֹדֶשׁ לְקֹדֶשׁ׃

Transliteration: Barukh atah Adonai, Eloheinu melekh ha-olam, ha-mavdil bein kodesh l'chol, bein or l'choshekh, bein Yisrael la-amim, bein yom ha-sh'vi'i l'sheshet y'mei ha-ma'aseh. Bein k'dushat Shabbat li-k'dushat Yom Tov hivdalta, v'et yom ha-sh'vi'i mi-sheshet y'mei ha-ma'aseh kidashta. Hivdalta v'kidashta et am'cha Yisrael bi-k'dushatecha. Barukh atah Adonai, ha-mavdil bein kodesh l'kodesh.

English: Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who distinguishes between sacred and secular, between light and darkness, between Israel and the nations, between the seventh day and the six days of work, and between the holiness of Shabbat and the holiness of Yom Tov. Blessed are You, Lord, who distinguishes between sacred and sacred.

2

Step 2

Urchatz

וּרְחַץ

Ritual handwashing before karpas.

How to do this step: Wash each hand three times with a cup. No blessing is said at this point.
3

Step 3

Karpas

כַּרְפַּס

Eat a small vegetable dipped in salt water.

How to do this step: Dip a small piece of karpas in salt water, say the blessing, and eat.

Borei Pri HaAdamah

What we are blessing: God as creator of produce from the earth.

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם בּוֹרֵא פְּרִי הָאֲדָמָה׃

Transliteration: Barukh atah Adonai, Eloheinu melekh ha-olam, borei p'ri ha-adamah.

English: Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, Creator of the fruit of the earth.

4

Step 4

Yachatz

יַחַץ

Break the middle matzah and hide the larger piece for afikoman.

How to do this step: Break the middle matzah. Return the smaller part to the stack. Wrap/hide the larger part for later (Tzafun).
5

Step 5

Maggid

מַגִּיד

Tell the story of the Exodus. Fill cup #2 and begin the narrative.

How to do this step: This is the heart of the Seder. Tell the story, ask questions, discuss meaning, and involve everyone at the table.

Ha Lachma Anya (Opening)

הָא לַחְמָא עַנְיָא דִּי אֲכָלוּ אַבְהָתָנָא בְּאַרְעָא דְמִצְרָיִם. כָּל דִּכְפִין יֵיתֵי וְיֵיכוֹל. כָּל דִּצְרִיךְ יֵיתֵי וְיִפְסַח. הָשַׁתָּא הָכָא, לְשָׁנָה הַבָּאָה בְּאַרְעָא דְיִשְׂרָאֵל. הָשַׁתָּא עַבְדֵי, לְשָׁנָה הַבָּאָה בְּנֵי חוֹרִין׃
Ha lachma anya di achalu avhatana b'ara d'Mitzrayim. Kol dichfin yeitei v'yeichol. Kol ditzrich yeitei v'yifsach. Hashata hacha, l'shana haba'a b'ara d'Yisrael. Hashata avdei, l'shana haba'a b'nei chorin.
This is the bread of affliction our ancestors ate in Egypt. Whoever is hungry, let them come and eat. Whoever needs, let them come and celebrate Pesach. This year we are here; next year in the Land of Israel. This year we are servants; next year free people.

Ma Nishtanah (The Four Questions)

מַה נִּשְׁתַּנָּה הַלַּיְלָה הַזֶּה מִכָּל הַלֵּילוֹת?
Mah nishtanah ha-laylah ha-zeh mi-kol ha-leilot?
What makes this night different from all other nights?
Call And Response
1
שֶׁבְּכָל הַלֵּילוֹת אָנוּ אוֹכְלִין חָמֵץ וּמַצָּה, הַלַּיְלָה הַזֶּה — כֻּלּוֹ מַצָּה.
She-b'chol ha-leilot anu ochlin chametz u-matzah, ha-laylah ha-zeh kulo matzah.
On all other nights we eat chametz or matzah; on this night, only matzah.
2
שֶׁבְּכָל הַלֵּילוֹת אָנוּ אוֹכְלִין שְׁאָר יְרָקוֹת, הַלַּיְלָה הַזֶּה — מָרוֹר.
She-b'chol ha-leilot anu ochlin sh'ar y'rakot, ha-laylah ha-zeh maror.
On all other nights we eat all kinds of vegetables; on this night, maror.
3
שֶׁבְּכָל הַלֵּילוֹת אֵין אָנוּ מַטְבִּילִין אֲפִילוּ פַּעַם אֶחָת, הַלַּיְלָה הַזֶּה — שְׁתֵּי פְעָמִים.
She-b'chol ha-leilot ein anu matbilin afilu pa'am echat, ha-laylah ha-zeh sh'tei f'amim.
On all other nights we do not dip even once; on this night, we dip twice.
4
שֶׁבְּכָל הַלֵּילוֹת אָנוּ אוֹכְלִין בֵּין יוֹשְׁבִין וּבֵין מְסֻבִּין, הַלַּיְלָה הַזֶּה — כֻּלָּנוּ מְסֻבִּין.
She-b'chol ha-leilot anu ochlin bein yoshvin u-vein m'subin, ha-laylah ha-zeh kulanu m'subin.
On all other nights we eat either sitting upright or reclining; on this night, we all recline.

Asher Ge'alanu (Redemption Blessing)

What we are blessing: God who redeemed us and our ancestors, bringing us to this Seder night.

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם, אֲשֶׁר גְּאָלָנוּ וְגָאַל אֶת־אֲבוֹתֵינוּ מִמִּצְרַיִם, וְהִגִּיעָנוּ הַלַּיְלָה הַזֶּה לֶאֱכָל־בּוֹ מַצָּה וּמָרוֹר. כֵּן יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ וֵאלֹהֵי אֲבוֹתֵינוּ יַגִּיעֵנוּ לְמוֹעֲדִים וְלִרְגָלִים אֲחֵרִים הַבָּאִים לִקְרָאתֵנוּ לְשָׁלוֹם, שְׂמֵחִים בְּבִנְיַן עִירֶךָ וְשָׂשִׂים בַּעֲבוֹדָתֶךָ. וְנֹאכַל שָׁם מִן הַזְּבָחִים וּמִן הַפְּסָחִים אֲשֶׁר יַגִּיעַ דָּמָם עַל קִיר מִזְבַּחֲךָ לְרָצוֹן, וְנוֹדֶה לְךָ שִׁיר חָדָש עַל גְּאֻלָּתֵנוּ וְעַל פְּדוּת נַפְשֵׁנוּ. בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ, גָּאַל יִשְׂרָאֵל׃

Transliteration: Barukh atah Adonai Eloheinu melekh ha-olam, asher ge'alanu v'ga'al et avoteinu mi-Mitzrayim, v'higi'anu ha-laylah ha-zeh le'echol bo matzah u-maror. Ken Adonai Eloheinu ve-Elohei avoteinu yagi'enu l'mo'adim v'l'r'galim acheirim ha-ba'im likrateinu l'shalom, s'meichim b'vinyan irecha v'sasim ba-avodatecha. V'nochal sham min ha-z'vachim u-min ha-p'sachim asher yagi'a damam al kir mizbachecha l'ratzon, v'nodeh l'cha shir chadash al ge'ulateinu v'al p'dut nafshenu. Barukh atah Adonai, ga'al Yisrael.

English: Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who redeemed us and our ancestors from Egypt and brought us to this night to eat matzah and maror. So too may You bring us to future festivals in peace, rejoicing in the rebuilding of Your city and in Your service. Blessed are You, Lord, Redeemer of Israel.

Second Cup: Borei Pri HaGafen

How: Conclude Maggid with this blessing and drink cup #2 while reclining left.

What we are blessing: Creator of the fruit of the vine.

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם בּוֹרֵא פְּרִי הַגָּפֶן׃

Transliteration: Barukh atah Adonai, Eloheinu melekh ha-olam, borei p'ri ha-gafen.

English: Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, Creator of the fruit of the vine.

Step 10

Korech

כּוֹרֵךְ

Hillel sandwich: matzah + maror together.

How to do this step: Make a sandwich from matzah and maror (often with charoset), recite the paragraph below, and eat while reclining.

Zecher L’Mikdash K’Hillel

זֵכֶר לְמִקְדָּשׁ כְּהִלֵּל. כֵּן עָשָׂה הִלֵּל בִּזְמַן שֶׁבֵּית הַמִּקְדָּשׁ הָיָה קַיָּם: הָיָה כּוֹרְכָן וְאוֹכְלָן בְּיַחַד, לְקַיֵּם מַה שֶּׁנֶּאֱמַר “עַל מַצּוֹת וּמְרֹרִים יֹאכְלֻהוּ”.
Zecher l'Mikdash k'Hillel. Ken asah Hillel bizman she-beit ha-Mikdash hayah kayam: hayah korechan v'ochlan b'yachad, l'kayem mah she-ne'emar: "al matzot u-m'rorim yochluhu."
In remembrance of the Temple practice according to Hillel: he would combine matzah and maror and eat them together, to fulfill "They shall eat it with matzot and bitter herbs."

Step 11

Shulchan Orech

שֻׁלְחָן עוֹרֵךְ

Festive meal.

How to do this step: Serve the meal. Keep room for afikoman. Avoid foods that are eaten after afikoman later in the night.

Step 12

Tzafun

צָפוּן

Eat afikoman (the hidden matzah).

How to do this step: Eat the afikoman while reclining left. This is the last food of the evening (besides the final cups).
13

Step 13

Barech

בָּרֵךְ

Birkat HaMazon (Grace after Meals) and cup #3.

How to do this step: Fill cup #3. Recite Birkat HaMazon. Then say the wine blessing and drink while reclining left.

Birkat HaMazon Core Blessings

1) HaZan Et HaKol

What we are blessing: God who sustains the world with food.

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ, אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם, הַזָּן אֶת הָעוֹלָם כֻּלּוֹ בְּטוּבוֹ בְּחֵן בְּחֶסֶד וּבְרַחֲמִים, הוּא נוֹתֵן לֶחֶם לְכָל בָּשָׂר כִּי לְעוֹלָם חַסְדוֹ. וּבְטוּבוֹ הַגָּדוֹל תָּמִיד לֹא חָסַר לָנוּ, וְאַל יֶחְסַר לָנוּ מָזוֹן לְעוֹלָם וָעֶד. בַּעֲבוּר שְׁמוֹ הַגָּדוֹל, כִּי הוּא אֵל זָן וּמְפַרְנֵס לַכֹּל וּמֵטִיב לַכֹּל, וּמֵכִין מָזוֹן לְכָל בְּרִיּוֹתָיו אֲשֶׁר בָּרָא. בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ, הַזָּן אֶת הַכֹּל׃

Transliteration: Barukh atah Adonai, Eloheinu melekh ha-olam, ha-zan et ha-olam kulo b'tuvo, b'chen, b'chesed u-v'rachamim. Hu noten lechem l'chol basar ki l'olam chasdo. U-v'tuvo ha-gadol tamid lo chasar lanu, v'al yechsar lanu mazon l'olam va'ed. Ba'avur sh'mo ha-gadol, ki hu El zan u-m'farneis la-kol u-meitiv la-kol, u-meichin mazon l'chol b'riyotav asher bara. Barukh atah Adonai, ha-zan et ha-kol.

English: Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who nourishes the whole world in goodness, grace, kindness, and mercy. Blessed are You, Lord, who nourishes all.

2) Al HaAretz Ve'Al HaMazon

What we are blessing: Gratitude for the Land and for food.

נוֹדֶה לְךָ יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ עַל שֶׁהִנְחַלְתָּ לַאֲבוֹתֵינוּ אֶרֶץ חֶמְדָה טוֹבָה וּרְחָבָה, וְעַל שֶׁהוֹצֵאתָנוּ יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֵאֶרֶץ מִצְרַיִם, וּפְדִיתָנוּ מִבֵּית עֲבָדִים, וְעַל בְּרִיתְךָ שֶׁחָתַמְתָּ בִּבְשָׂרֵנוּ, וְעַל תּוֹרָתְךָ שֶׁלִּמַּדְתָּנוּ, וְעַל חֻקֶּיךָ שֶׁהוֹדַעְתָּנוּ, וְעַל חַיִּים חֵן וָחֶסֶד שֶׁחוֹנַנְתָּנוּ, וְעַל אֲכִילַת מָזוֹן שָׁאַתָּה זָן וּמְפַרְנֵס אוֹתָנוּ תָּמִיד, בְּכָל יוֹם וּבְכָל עֵת וּבְכָל שָׁעָה. וְעַל הַכֹּל יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ, אֲנַחְנוּ מוֹדִים לָךְ וּמְבָרְכִים אוֹתָךְ, יִתְבָּרַךְ שִׁמְךָ בְּפִי כָּל חַי תָּמִיד לְעוֹלָם וָעֶד. כַּכָּתוּב: וְאָכַלְתָּ וְשָׂבַעְתָּ וּבֵרַכְתָּ אֶת־יְיָ אֱלֹהֶיךָ עַל־הָאָרֶץ הַטּוֹבָה אֲשֶּׁר נָתַן־לָךְ. בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ, עַל הָאָרֶץ וְעַל הַמָּזוֹן׃

Transliteration: Nodeh l'cha Adonai Eloheinu al she-hinchalta la-avoteinu eretz chemdah tovah u-r'chavah, v'al she-hotzeitanu Adonai Eloheinu me-eretz Mitzrayim, u-f'ditanu mi-beit avadim, v'al b'ritecha she-chatamta bivsareinu, v'al Toratecha she-limad'tanu, v'al chukecha she-hodatanu, v'al chayim chen va-chesed she-chonantanu, v'al achilat mazon she-atah zan u-m'farneis otanu tamid, b'chol yom u-v'chol et u-v'chol sha'ah. V'al ha-kol Adonai Eloheinu, anachnu modim lach u-m'var'chim otach, yitbarakh shimcha b'fi kol chai tamid l'olam va'ed. Ka-katuv: v'achalta v'savata u-veirachta et Adonai Elohecha al ha-aretz ha-tovah asher natan lach. Barukh atah Adonai, al ha-aretz v'al ha-mazon.

English: We thank You for the good land You gave our ancestors, for the Exodus from Egypt, for covenant and Torah, and for the food by which You sustain us at all times. Blessed are You, Lord, for the land and for the food.

3) Boneh Yerushalayim

What we are blessing: Prayer for mercy on Israel and the rebuilding of Jerusalem.

רַחֵם נָא יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ עַל יִשְׂרָאֵל עַמֶּךָ וְעַל יְרוּשָׁלַיִם עִירֶךָ וְעַל צִיּוֹן מִשְׁכַּן כְּבוֹדֶךָ וְעַל מַלְכוּת בֵּית דָּוִד מְשִׁיחֶךָ וְעַל הַבַּיִת הַגָּדוֹל וְהַקָּדוֹשׁ שֶׁנִּקְרָא שִׁמְךָ עָלָיו: אֱלֹהֵינוּ אָבִינוּ, רְעֵנוּ זוּנֵנוּ פַּרְנְסֵנוּ וְכַלְכְּלֵנוּ וְהַרְוִיחֵנוּ, וְהַרְוַח לָנוּ יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ מְהֵרָה מִכָּל צָרוֹתֵינוּ. וְנָא אַל תַּצְרִיכֵנוּ יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ, לֹא לִידֵי מַתְּנַת בָּשָׂר וָדָם וְלֹא לִידֵי הַלְוָאָתָם, כִּי אִם לְיָדְךָ הַמְּלֵאָה הַפְּתוּחָה הַקְּדוֹשָׁה וְהָרְחָבָה, שֶׁלֹא נֵבוֹשׁ וְלֹא נִכָּלֵם לְעוֹלָם וָעֶד. His hosts answer: Yours and Yours alone; Yours, O Lord, is the kingdom.
דָּגוּל בִּמְלוּכָה, הָדוּר כַּהֲלָכָה.
Dagul bi-meluchah, hadur ka-halakhah.
Renowned in kingship, glorious in perfection.
וָתִיקָיו יֹאמְרוּ לוֹ: לְךָ וּלְךָ... לְךָ יְיָ הַמַּמְלָכָה.
Vatikav yom'ru lo: l'cha u-l'cha... l'cha Adonai ha-mamlachah.
His elders answer: Yours and Yours alone; Yours, O Lord, is the kingdom.
זַכַּאי בִּמְלוּכָה, חָסִין כַּהֲלָכָה.
Zakkai bi-meluchah, chasin ka-halakhah.
Worthy in kingship, mighty in perfection.

Yachid bi-meluchah, kabir ka-halakhah.
Unique in kingship, mighty in perfection.

Moshel bi-meluchah, nora ka-halakhah.
Ruler in kingship, awesome in perfection.

Anav bi-meluchah, podeh ka-halakhah.
Humble in kingship, redeemer in perfection.

Kadosh bi-meluchah, rachum ka-halakhah.

Takif bi-meluchah, tomekh ka-halakhah.
