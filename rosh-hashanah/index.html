About the Holiday

Introduction

What is the Rosh Hashanah Seder?

מהו סדר ראש השנה

Rosh Hashanah is the holiday that opens the Hebrew year and is also the Day of Judgment, when our fate for the coming year is decided. The name means "Head of the Year," and it marks the beginning of the High Holy Days — the most spiritually significant period of the Jewish calendar.

Despite the serious atmosphere of judgment, we hold joyful festive meals and express hope for a better year. The Rosh Hashanah Seder features a series of symbolic foods — each connected to a special prayer — that express our deepest hopes for the year ahead. The Hebrew or Aramaic name of each food connects to the theme of its prayer by sound or meaning.
Opening the Evening

Step 1

Kiddush & Opening Blessings

קִידּוּשׁ וּבִרְכּוֹת הַפְּתִיחָה

Preparation: Fill a Kiddush cup with wine or grape juice. The leader holds the cup and recites the following blessings.

Kiddush for Rosh Hashanah

Blessing over the Wine

Recited while holding the Kiddush cup.

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה ה' אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם, בּוֹרֵא פְּרִי הַגָּפֶן:

Baruch atah Adonai, Eloheinu Melech ha'olam, borei p'ri hagafen.

Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who creates the fruit of the vine.

Kiddush — Sanctifying the Day

This blessing sanctifies Rosh Hashanah as the "Day of Remembrance."

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה ה' אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם, אֲשֶׁר בָּחַר בָּנוּ מִכָּל עָם, וְרוֹמְמָנוּ מִכָּל לָשׁוֹן, וְקִדְּשָׁנוּ בְּמִצְוֹתָיו. וַתִּתֶּן לָנוּ ה' אֱלוֹהֵינוּ בְּאַהֲבָה אֶת יוֹם הַזִּכָּרוֹן, יוֹם זִכְרוֹן תְּרוּעָה בְּאַהֲבָה מִקְרָא קֹדֶשׁ, זֵכֶר לִיצִיאַת מִצְרָיִם. כִּי בָנוּ בָחַרְתָּ וְאוֹתָנוּ קִדַּשְׁתָּ מִכָּל הָעַמִים, וּדְבָרְךָ אֱמֶת וְקַיָּם לָעַד. בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה ה', מֶלֶךְ עַל כֹּל הָאָרֶץ, מְקַדֵּשׁ יִשְׂרָאֵל וְיוֹם הַזִּכָּרוֹן:

Baruch atah Adonai, Eloheinu Melech ha'olam, asher bachar banu mikol am, v'rom'manu mikol lashon, v'kid'shanu b'mitzvotav. Vatiten lanu Adonai Eloheinu b'ahavah et yom ha-zikaron, yom zichron t'ruah b'ahavah mikra kodesh, zecher litziat Mitzrayim. Ki vanu vacharta v'otanu kidashta mikol ha'amim, u-d'varecha emet v'kayam la'ad. Baruch atah Adonai, Melech al kol ha'aretz, m'kadesh Yisrael v'yom ha-zikaron.

Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who has chosen us from all peoples, exalted us above every tongue, and sanctified us with His commandments. You gave us, Lord our God, with love, this Day of Remembrance — a day of memory and the sounding of the shofar — a holy convocation, a memorial of the Exodus from Egypt. For You have chosen us and sanctified us from all the nations. Blessed are You, Lord, King over all the earth, who sanctifies Israel and the Day of Remembrance.

Everyone drinks from the wine or grape juice.

Shehecheyanu — Who Has Kept Us Alive

Shehecheyanu

Recited on the first night to thank God for bringing us to this season.

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה ה', אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם, שֶׁהֶחֱיָנוּ וְקִיְּמָנוּ וְהִגִּיעָנוּ לַזְּמַן הַזֶּה:

Baruch atah Adonai, Eloheinu Melech ha'olam, shehecheyanu v'kiy'manu v'higiyanu lazman hazeh.

Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who has kept us alive, sustained us, and brought us to this season.

Washing Hands

Go to a sink and wash hands by pouring water over the right hand, then the left. Recite the blessing before drying your hands.

Al Netilat Yadayim — Washing of Hands

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה ה' אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם, אֲשֶׁר קִדְּשָׁנוּ בְּמִצְוֹתָיו וְצִוָּנוּ עַל נְטִילַת יָדָיִם:

Baruch atah Adonai, Eloheinu Melech ha'olam, asher kid'shanu b'mitzvotav v'tzivanu al netilat yadayim.

Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who has sanctified us with His commandments and commanded us concerning the washing of hands.

Blessing on the Challah

The leader holds the round challah, recites the blessing, cuts or breaks it. A piece is dipped in honey and passed to everyone.

Ha-Motzi — Blessing over Bread

On Rosh Hashanah the challah is round, symbolizing the cycle of the year and a sweet year ahead.

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה ה' אֱלֹהֵינוּ מֶלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם, הַמּוֹצִיא לֶחֶם מִן הָאָרֶץ:

Baruch atah Adonai, Eloheinu Melech ha'olam, ha-motzi lechem min ha'aretz.

Blessed are You, Lord our God, King of the universe, who brings forth bread from the earth.

The Symbolic Foods

Seder Simanim

The Symbols of Rosh Hashanah

סִימָנֵי רֹאשׁ הַשָּׁנָה

Each symbolic food is accompanied by a special Yehi Ratzon prayer expressing our hopes for the New Year. The name of each food connects — by sound or meaning — to the theme of its prayer.

תָּמָר

Date (Tamar)

The Meaning: The prayer asks that our enemies "will come to an end" (sheyitamu) — a play on the word for date, tamar. We eat the date to symbolize hope for the end of all animosity.

Take a date, recite the blessing over fruit, take a bite, then say the Yehi Ratzon.

Yehi Ratzon

יְהִי רָצוֹן מִלְפָנֶיךָ ה' אֱלֹהֵינוּ וֵאלֹהֵי אֲבוֹתֵינוּ שֶׁיִּתַּמּוּ אוֹיְבֵינוּ וְשׂנְאֵינוּ וְכָל מְבַקְשֵׁי רָעָתֵנוּ:

Yehi ratzon milfanecha Adonai Eloheinu v'Elohei avoteinu, sheyitamu oyveinu v'soneinu v'chol m'vakshei ra'ateinu.

May it be Your will, Lord our God and God of our fathers, that our enemies, our haters, and all who seek our harm will come to an end.

רִמּוֹן

Pomegranate (Rimon)

The Meaning: A pomegranate is filled with hundreds of seeds. We eat it while praying that our merits in the new year will be as numerous as its seeds.

Take some pomegranate seeds and say the following.

Yehi Ratzon

יְהִי רָצוֹן מִלְפָנֶיךָ ה' אֱלֹהֵינוּ וֵאלֹהֵי אֲבוֹתֵינוּ שֶׁנִּהְיֶה מְלֵאִים מִצְווֹת כָּרִמּוֹן.

Yehi ratzon milfanecha Adonai Eloheinu v'Elohei avoteinu, she'nihiyeh m'leim mitzvot ka'rimon.

May it be Your will, Lord our God and God of our fathers, that we be as full of mitzvot as the pomegranate.

קָרָא

Gourd / Pumpkin (Kara)

The Meaning: The Aramaic name K'ra sounds like the Hebrew for "to tear" and "to be read." We ask that God tear up negative decrees and that our merits be read before Him.

Take a piece of cooked gourd, recite the blessing over vegetables, take a bite, then say the Yehi Ratzon.

Yehi Ratzon

יְהִי רָצוֹן מִלְפָנֶיךָ ה' אֱלֹהֵינוּ וֵאלֹהֵי אֲבוֹתֵינוּ שֶׁתִּקְרַע רֹעַ גְּזַר דִּינֵנוּ, וְיִקָּרְאוּ לְפָנֶיךָ זְכֻיּוֹתֵינוּ.

Yehi ratzon milfanecha Adonai Eloheinu v'Elohei avoteinu, she'tikra ro'a g'zar dinenu, v'yikare'u l'fanecha z'chuyoteinu.

May it be Your will, Lord our God and God of our fathers, that You tear up the evil of our verdict and may our merits be read before You.

רוּבִּיָּא

Rubia (Fenugreek / Beans)

The Meaning: The Aramaic name Rubia sounds like the Hebrew word for "increase" (ribui). We eat it to hope that our merits will increase and abound.

Take a bite after saying the following.

Yehi Ratzon

יְהִי רָצוֹן מִלְפָנֶיךָ ה' אֱלֹהֵינוּ וֵאלֹהֵי אֲבוֹתֵינוּ שֶׁיִּרְבּוּ זְכֻיּוֹתֵינוּ וּתְרַבֶּה הַבְּרָכָה בְּכָל עֲמָלֵנוּ.

Yehi ratzon milfanecha Adonai Eloheinu v'Elohei avoteinu, she'yirbu z'chuyoteinu u'trabeh ha'bracha b'chol amaleinu.

May it be Your will, Lord our God and God of our fathers, that our merits increase and that blessing abound in all our labors.

כַּרְתִּי

Leek (Karti)

The Meaning: The Aramaic name Karti sounds like the Hebrew verb "to cut off" (yikartu). We eat this hoping that our enemies will be "cut off."

Take a bite of cooked leek after saying the following.

Yehi Ratzon

יְהִי רָצוֹן מִלְפָנֶיךָ ה' אֱלֹהֵינוּ וֵאלֹהֵי אֲבוֹתֵינוּ שֶׁיִּכָּרְתוּ אוֹיְבֵינוּ וְכָל מְבַקְשֵׁי רָעָתֵנוּ.

Yehi ratzon milfanecha Adonai Eloheinu v'Elohei avoteinu, she'yikartu oyveinu v'chol m'vakshei ra'ateinu.

May it be Your will, Lord our God and God of our fathers, that our enemies and all who seek our harm be cut off.

סֶלֶק

Beet (Selek)

The Meaning: The Hebrew word Selek sounds like the word for "remove" (yistalku). We ask that our sins and adversaries be "removed" from before us.

Take a bite of cooked beet and say the following.

Yehi Ratzon

יְהִי רָצוֹן מִלְפָנֶיךָ ה' אֱלֹהֵינוּ וֵאלֹהֵי אֲבוֹתֵינוּ שֶׁיִּסְתַּלְּקוּ עֲוֹנוֹתֵינוּ וְשׂנְאֵינוּ.

Yehi ratzon milfanecha Adonai Eloheinu v'Elohei avoteinu, she'yistaleku avonoteinu v'soneinu.

May it be Your will, Lord our God and God of our fathers, that our iniquities be removed, and our enemies as well.

תַּפּוּחַ בִּדְבַשׁ

Apple in Honey

The Meaning: Perhaps the most beloved Rosh Hashanah ritual. The apple's sweetness combined with honey is a direct symbol for our hope for a "good and sweet year."

Take a slice of apple, dip it in honey, and say the following before eating.

Yehi Ratzon

יְהִי רָצוֹן מִלְּפָנֶיךָ יְיָ אֱלֹהֵינוּ וֵאלֹהֵי אֲבוֹתֵינוּ שֶׁתְּחַדֵּשׁ עָלֵינוּ שָׁנָה טוֹבָה וּמְתוּקָה.

Yehi ratzon milfanecha Adonai Eloheinu v'Elohei avoteinu, she't'chadesh aleinu shanah tovah u'm'tukah.

May it be Your will, Lord our God and God of our fathers, that You renew for us a good and sweet year.

רֹאשׁ שֶׁל דָּג

Head of a Fish

The Meaning: The Hebrew word for "head" is Rosh — the same word that opens the holiday's name, Rosh Hashanah. We eat from the head to symbolize our desire to be "a head and not a tail" — leaders, not followers — in the year to come.

Take a small piece from the head of the fish and say the following.

Yehi Ratzon

יְהִי רָצוֹן מִלִּפְנֵי אָבִינוּ שֶׁבַּשָּׁמַיִם, שֶׁנִּהְיֶה לְרֹאשׁ וְלֹא לְזָנָב, שֶׁנִּפְרֶה וְנִרְבֶּה כַּדָּגִים.

Yehi ratzon milifnei avinu shebashamayim, she'nihiyeh l'rosh v'lo l'zanav, she'nifreh v'nirbeh ka'dagim.

May it be the will of our Father in Heaven that we be as a head and not as a tail, and that we be fruitful and multiply like fish.

Prayers for Our Soldiers & Hostages

תְּפִלּוֹת לְשָׁלוֹם חַיָּלֵי צָה"ל וְהַשְׁבַּת הַחֲטוּפִים

Psalm 121 · תְּהִלִּים קכ"א

שִׁיר לַמַּעֲלוֹת אֶשָּׂא עֵינַי אֶל־הֶהָרִים מֵאַיִן יָבֹא עֶזְרִי׃ עֶזְרִי מֵעִם יְהֹוָה עֹשֵׂה שָׁמַיִם וָאָרֶץ׃ אַל־יִתֵּן לַמּוֹט רַגְלֶךָ אַל־יָנוּם שֹׁמְרֶךָ׃ הִנֵּה לֹא־יָנוּם וְלֹא יִישָׁן שׁוֹמֵר יִשְׂרָאֵל׃ יְהֹוָה שֹׁמְרֶךָ יְהֹוָה צִלְּךָ עַל־יַד יְמִינֶךָ׃ יוֹמָם הַשֶּׁמֶשׁ לֹא יַכֶּכָּה וְיָרֵחַ בַּלָּיְלָה׃ יְהֹוָה יִשְׁמׇרְךָ מִכָּל־רָע יִשְׁמֹר אֶת־נַפְשֶׁךָ׃ יְהֹוָה יִשְׁמׇר צֵאתְךָ וּבוֹאֶךָ מֵעַתָּה וְעַד־עוֹלָם׃

A song for ascents. I turn my eyes to the mountains; from where will my help come? My help comes from the Lord, maker of heaven and earth. He will not let your foot give way; your guardian will not slumber. See, the guardian of Israel neither slumbers nor sleeps! The Lord is your guardian, the Lord is your protection at your right hand. By day the sun will not strike you, nor the moon by night. The Lord will guard you from all harm; He will guard your life. The Lord will guard your going and coming now and forever.

Prayer for the IDF Soldiers · תְּפִלָּה לְשָׁלוֹם חַיָּלֵי צָה"ל

מִי שֶׁבֵּרַךְ אֲבוֹתֵינוּ אַבְרָהָם יִצְחָק וְיַעֲקֹב, הוּא יְבָרֵךְ אֶת חַיָּלֵי צְבָא הֲגַנָּה לְיִשְׂרָאֵל, הָעוֹמְדִים עַל מִשְׁמַר אַרְצֵנוּ וְעָרֵי אֱלֹהֵינוּ. הַקָּדוֹשׁ בָּרוּךְ הוּא יִשְׁמֹר וְיַצִּיל אֶת חַיָּלֵינוּ מִכָּל צָרָה וְצוּקָה וּמִכָּל נֶגַע וּמַחְלָה, וְיִשְׁלַח בְּרָכָה וְהַצְלָחָה בְּכָל מַעֲשֵׂה יְדֵיהֶם. בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה ה' שׁוֹמֵר יִשְׂרָאֵל. וְנֹאמַר אָמֵן.

May He who blessed our fathers, Abraham, Isaac and Jacob, bless the soldiers of the Israel Defense Forces who stand guard over our land and the cities of our God. May the Holy One, blessed be He, guard and rescue our soldiers from all trouble and distress and from all affliction and illness, and send blessing and success in all the work of their hands. Blessed are You, Lord, Guardian of Israel. And let us say, Amen.

Prayer for Those Held in Captivity · תְּפִלָּה לְהַשְׁבַּת הַחֲטוּפִים

מִי שֶׁבֵּרַךְ אֲבוֹתֵינוּ, הוּא יְבָרֵךְ וְיִשְׁמֹר אֶת הַשְּׁבוּיִים בְּתוֹךְ שְׁאָר אֲחֵינוּ בֵּית יִשְׂרָאֵל הַנְּתוּנִים בְּצָרָה וּבְשִׁבְיָה. הַקָּדוֹשׁ בָּרוּךְ הוּא יִמָּלֵא רַחֲמִים עֲלֵיהֶם, וְיוֹצִיאֵם מֵחֹשֶׁךְ וְצַלְמָוֶת, וּמוֹסְרוֹתֵיהֶם יְנַתֵּק, וּמִמְּצוּקוֹתֵיהֶם יוֹשִׁיעֵם, וִישִׁיבֵם מְהֵרָה לְחֵיק מִשְׁפְּחוֹתֵיהֶם. "וּפְדוּיֵי ה' יְשׁוּבוּן וּבָאוּ צִיּוֹן בְּרִנָּה." וְנֹאמַר אָמֵן.

May He who blessed our fathers bless and protect the hostages amongst our brethren the House of Israel who are placed in trouble and in captivity. May the Holy One, blessed be He, have compassion upon them and bring them out from darkness and the shadow of death, break their bonds, deliver them from distress, and bring them safely back to their families' embrace. "Those redeemed by the Lord will return and come to Zion with singing." And let us say: Amen.
